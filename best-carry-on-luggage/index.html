Quick answer

Carry-on luggage is the sweet spot for most travelers: big enough for a week’s worth of clothes, small enough to skip baggage claim. Choosing the right one comes down to weight, wheels, handle height, and how it fits the tightest airline sizers. Hard-sided bags offer better protection against crush damage; soft-sided bags give you exterior pockets and more flexibility when you need to squeeze into a full bin. Whichever style you prefer, look for a smooth-gliding four-wheel spinner, a telescopic handle that locks firmly, and a warranty that covers wear and tear.

If you’re in the air every few weeks, the Travelpro Platinum Elite’s extra pockets, built-in USB port, and lifetime warranty add up to real convenience. Our pick: Travelpro Platinum Elite.

Best for budget travelers

When you’d rather spend your money on experiences than baggage, the Samsonite Omni PC gives you a hard-sided spinner at a very low price. It’s light, scratch-resistant, and expandable – all the practical features you need without the premium price tag. Just be gentle with the zippers and lining over time. Our pick: Samsonite Omni PC.

Best for style-conscious travelers

The Rimowa Essential Lite proves that luggage can be as much an accessory as a tool. Its grooved shell and lightweight feel turn heads at the gate. You pay a steep premium, but you get reliable performance and a five-year warranty that makes it feel less like a splurge and more like a long-term piece. Our pick: Rimowa Essential Lite.

Our verdict

The Away The Carry-On remains the safest bet for most people: it’s well-built, thoughtfully designed, and backed by a solid warranty. If you fly constantly, the Travelpro Platinum Elite’s organization and USB port justify the extra weight and cost. Budget shoppers will be thrilled with the Samsonite Omni PC, and style-focused travelers should spring for the Rimowa Essential Lite. For tight bins and overhead restrictions, the Béis The Carry-On Roller delivers compact convenience without sacrificing style. Whichever you choose, make sure it fits the airline you fly most – and happy travels.
