Shopping for carry-on luggage on a budget doesn’t mean settling for flimsy zippers or wobbly wheels. Airline size restrictions are tighter than ever, and a bag that’s too heavy or poorly built can turn a quick getaway into a headache. The right carry-on balances weight, durability, and maneuverability, and you don’t need to spend a fortune to get it. When you’re comparing options, look past the price tag. Check the material: polycarbonate hardshells resist impact for their weight, while ballistic nylon soft-sides often squeeze into tight overhead compartments. Four spinner wheels give you easy airport mobility, but two-wheel rollers tend to be more stable on uneven surfaces. Weight matters too, a lighter bag leaves more room for your stuff without hitting airline limits.
